### **PAGE 1: Overview, Key Characteristics, Signature Colors, Colors to Avoid**

**Overview:** You are a True Winter, characterized by your cool undertones and high natural contrast that creates a striking, sophisticated appearance. Your coloring typically features dark hair, cool-toned skin, and eyes that are often dark or strikingly light in contrast. Your ideal palette draws from winter's most elegant colors: deep burgundy, navy blue, emerald green, and crisp white. These colors enhance your natural drama and complement your cool undertones beautifully. Stay away from warm, golden, or muted colors that will clash with your cool coloring and make you appear sallow or tired.

**Key Characteristics:**

- **Undertone:** Cool (blue, pink, or neutral-cool undertones)
- **Contrast Level:** High (striking difference between hair, skin, and eye colors)
- **Chroma:** Clear & Saturated (can handle pure, undiluted colors)
- **Value:** Medium to Deep (looks best in colors with substance and depth)

**Signature Colors:**

- True Red (`#DC143C`) - Your power color that enhances your natural intensity
- Navy Blue (`#000080`) - Your perfect neutral that works as well as black
- Emerald Green (`#50C878`) - Brings out any green in your eyes and complements your cool tones
- Pure White (`#FFFFFF`) - Your ideal light neutral, crisp and clean
- Deep Burgundy (`#800020`) - Rich and sophisticated, perfect for formal occasions
- Royal Blue (`#4169E1`) - Enhances your natural regality and confidence
- Charcoal Grey (`#36454F`) - Your softer neutral alternative to black
- Fuchsia Pink (`#FF1493`) - Your best pink option with cool undertones

**Colors to Avoid:** Orange, Peach, Coral, Warm Yellow, Golden Brown, Sage Green, Dusty Rose, Beige, Taupe, Cream, Rust, Olive, Terracotta, Camel, Ivory

### **PAGE 2: Color Overview**

**Palette Description:** Your True Winter palette consists of clear, saturated colors with cool undertones. These colors are pure and undiluted, creating maximum impact against your high-contrast coloring. The palette includes the deepest, most sophisticated blues, the clearest reds, and the most vibrant greens. Your neutrals are crisp and clean - think pure white, charcoal grey, and navy blue rather than warm beiges or creams.

**Color Dimensions:**

- **Temperature:** Cool (blue-based undertones)
- **Saturation:** High (clear, pure colors)
- **Value Range:** Medium to Deep (avoiding very light pastels)
- **Intensity:** Bold and striking

### **PAGE 3: Clothing Overview**

**Your True Winter Wardrobe:** Build your wardrobe around your signature colors for maximum impact. Navy blue serves as your primary neutral - use it where others would use brown or beige. Black is excellent for formal wear, while charcoal grey offers a softer alternative. Your reds should be true and clear, your greens should be emerald or forest tones, and your blues should be royal or navy. Patterns work best when they incorporate your signature colors in high contrast combinations. Avoid anything with warm undertones, dusty finishes, or muted qualities.

### **PAGE 4: Metals, Jewelry, Watches and Glasses**

**Best Metals:**

- **Silver** - Your most flattering metal choice
- **Platinum** - Luxurious and perfectly cool-toned
- **White Gold** - Modern and sophisticated
- **Gunmetal** - Edgy alternative for casual wear

**Jewelry Style:** Choose pieces with clean lines and geometric shapes. Your jewelry should have the same clarity and boldness as your color palette. Avoid ornate, vintage, or overly decorative pieces that compete with your natural drama.

**Watches:** Opt for silver, white gold, or black metal watch faces. Leather bands should be black, navy, or deep burgundy - never brown or tan.

**Glasses:** Silver, gunmetal, black, or navy frames work best. Avoid gold, tortoiseshell, or warm brown frames. Bold, geometric shapes complement your high-contrast features.

### **PAGE 5: Hair Color**

**Natural Hair Colors:** True Winters typically have naturally dark hair - deep brown, black, or very dark blonde with cool undertones.

**Best Hair Color Options:**

- **Natural Black** - Classic and striking
- **Cool Dark Brown** - Rich without warmth
- **Ash Blonde** - If going lighter, keep it cool-toned
- **Silver/Grey** - Elegant and sophisticated when going natural
- **Bold Colors** - Deep burgundy, navy blue, or other signature colors for adventurous choices

**Colors to Avoid:** Warm browns, golden blonde, auburn, copper, or any hair color with golden or red undertones.

### **PAGE 6: Makeup**

**Your True Winter Makeup Palette:** Your makeup should be as bold and clear as your clothing colors. Think dramatic eyes, true red lips, and clean, cool-toned base products. You can handle intense makeup that would overwhelm other seasons.
