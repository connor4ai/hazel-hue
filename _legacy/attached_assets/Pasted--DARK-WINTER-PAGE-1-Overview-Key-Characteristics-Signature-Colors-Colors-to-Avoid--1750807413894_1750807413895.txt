## DARK WINTER

### **PAGE 1: Overview, Key Characteristics, Signature Colors, Colors to Avoid**

**Overview:** As a Dark Winter, you have deep, intense coloring with cool undertones that can carry the richest, most sophisticated colors. Your features are characterized by high contrast and depth - typically dark hair, eyes that are deep or strikingly light, and skin with cool undertones. Your palette consists of the deepest, most luxurious colors: midnight blue, deep burgundy, forest green, and charcoal. These colors enhance your natural intensity and create an aura of elegance and power. Avoid light, warm, or overly bright colors that will overwhelm your sophisticated natural coloring and appear garish against your refined features.

**Key Characteristics:**

- **Undertone:** Cool (blue or neutral-cool undertones)
- **Contrast Level:** High (but deeper than Bright Winter)
- **Chroma:** Deep & Rich (sophisticated, darker colors)
- **Value:** Deep to Medium (avoiding very light colors)

**Signature Colors:**

- Deep Burgundy (`#722F37`) - Your most sophisticated color
- Midnight Blue (`#191970`) - Dark and mysterious
- Forest Green (`#355E3B`) - Rich and luxurious
- Charcoal Grey (`#36454F`) - Your perfect neutral
- Deep Purple (`#301934`) - Regal and elegant
- Black (`#000000`) - Classic and timeless
- Pine Green (`#01796F`) - Deep and natural
- Deep Magenta (`#8B008B`) - Bold yet sophisticated

**Colors to Avoid:** Light colors, Pastels, Bright Yellow, Orange, Peach, Coral, Light Pink, Sage Green, Beige, Taupe, Cream, Golden colors, Rust, Light Blue

### **PAGE 2: Color Overview**

**Palette Description:** Your Dark Winter palette consists of the deepest, most sophisticated colors with cool undertones. These colors have weight and gravitas, creating an atmosphere of luxury and elegance. Your palette includes the darkest blues, the deepest reds, and the richest greens. Unlike Bright Winter's electric colors, your colors are deep and mysterious, like precious gemstones.

**Color Dimensions:**

- **Temperature:** Cool (blue-based undertones)
- **Saturation:** Deep and rich
- **Value Range:** Deep to medium (avoiding light values)
- **Intensity:** Sophisticated and powerful

### **PAGE 3: Clothing Overview**

**Your Dark Winter Wardrobe:** Your wardrobe should exude sophistication and luxury. Build around deep, rich colors that create a sense of mystery and elegance. Black is excellent, but don't overlook deep burgundy and midnight blue as equally powerful alternatives. Your patterns should be rich and complex - think paisley, deep florals, or geometric patterns in your signature colors.

### **PAGE 4: Metals, Jewelry, Watches and Glasses**

**Best Metals:**

- **Silver** - Classic and elegant
- **Platinum** - Luxurious and sophisticated
- **White Gold** - Modern elegance
- **Gunmetal** - Dark and mysterious
- **Oxidized Silver** - Adds depth and richness

**Jewelry Style:** Choose substantial, sophisticated pieces with rich details. Your jewelry can be more ornate than other Winter types, as long as it maintains elegance.

**Watches:** Dark metals, black leather bands, or deep burgundy leather. Sophisticated complications and details work well.

**Glasses:** Dark frames - black, gunmetal, deep burgundy, or dark tortoiseshell (if it's cool-toned).

### **PAGE 5: Hair Color**

**Natural Hair Colors:** Dark Winters typically have naturally dark hair - deep brown or black.

**Best Hair Color Options:**

- **Natural Black** - Timeless and sophisticated
- **Deep Cool Brown** - Rich and elegant
- **Dark Burgundy** - Adds richness while staying cool
- **Deep Purple** - For bold, sophisticated looks
- **Silver/Grey** - Elegant and distinguished

**Colors to Avoid:** Light colors, warm browns, golden highlights, auburn, copper, or any warm tones.
